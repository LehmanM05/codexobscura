# Rogue Guide

## Introduction:
Few archetypes are as enduring as the rogue.

From the calculated patience of [Garrett](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Thief_(series)), to the lethal efficiency of [Arya Stark](https://awoiaf.westeros.org/index.php/Arya_Stark), to the silver-tongued audacity of [Locke Lamora](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/The_Lies_of_Locke_Lamora), the rogue fantasy is not about brute force. It is about leverage.

The rogue wins through positioning. Through timing. Through knowing something the enemy does not.

In **Pathfinder Second Edition**, the Rogue is one of the most tactically expressive classes in the system. It is not simply “the stealth class,” nor is it just a damage chassis for Sneak Attack. The Rogue is a precision striker, a battlefield opportunist, and arguably the most skill-dominant class in the game.

It rewards planning. It rewards awareness. It rewards system mastery.

And when piloted well, it is devastating.

---

### The Rogue’s True Role

At a glance, the Rogue appears straightforward:
- Apply [Off-Guard](https://2e.aonprd.com/Conditions.aspx?ID=58)
- Trigger [Sneak Attack](https://2e.aonprd.com/Classes.aspx?ID=37)
- Deal extra damage

But this surface-level reading undersells the class.

The Rogue’s true strength lies in action efficiency and information control. It exploits small advantages better than almost any other class. A flanked enemy is not merely easier to hit — it becomes a liability. A successful Recall Knowledge check is not flavor — it is a damage trigger. A Demoralize action is not filler — it is the beginning of a cascade.

Where other classes force advantage, the Rogue capitalizes on it.

This distinction matters.

Rogues are not independent damage engines in the way Fighters or Barbarians can be. They are synergy amplifiers. They turn teamwork into precision damage. They convert battlefield chaos into calculated execution.

If you enjoy reading the field, manipulating conditions, and squeezing value out of every action, the Rogue is one of the most rewarding classes to master.

## Who Should Play a Rogue?

Play a Rogue if you:
- Enjoy tactical positioning and battlefield awareness
- Want the most skill flexibility in the system
- Prefer precision over brute force
- Like turning small advantages into decisive victories
- Want a class that scales with player mastery

Avoid the Rogue if you:
- Want to stand toe-to-toe and trade hits
- Prefer straightforward, repeatable rotations
- Dislike relying on positioning or team coordination

The Rogue thrives in capable hands. It punishes autopilot.

But for players willing to engage with its depth, it offers one of the most satisfying mechanical experiences in Pathfinder 2e.

## General Feats
##### [Adopted Ancestry](https://2e.aonprd.com/Feats.aspx?ID=5115)
Learn to be apart of another ancestry you have access to. Can select those ancestry feats as well as your original ancestry. Can lead to some fun shenanigans and strong interactions.
##### [Armor Proficiency](https://2e.aonprd.com/Feats.aspx?ID=5120)
Become trained in the next tier of armor, then at level 13 become an expert.
##### [Breath Control](https://2e.aonprd.com/Feats.aspx?ID=5129)
Hold your breath longer and gain benefits against inhaled threats. 
##### [Canny Acumen](https://2e.aonprd.com/Feats.aspx?ID=5130)
Become an expert in a saving throw or Perception. Shore up any weak saves.
##### [Diehard](https://2e.aonprd.com/Feats.aspx?ID=5140)
Die at dying 5, rather than dying 4. Handy if you're in melee.
##### [Fast Recovery](https://2e.aonprd.com/Feats.aspx?ID=5148)
Recover from disease faster, gain more HP from resting.
##### [Feather Step](https://2e.aonprd.com/Feats.aspx?ID=5149)
Gain the ability to Step into difficult terrain.
##### [Fleet](https://2e.aonprd.com/Feats.aspx?ID=5150)
Gain an extra 5 feet of speed. Not exciting, but hey, it helps to get into position.
##### [Incredible Initiative](https://2e.aonprd.com/Feats.aspx?ID=5160)
Gain a +2 circumstance bonus to initiative rolls. Not exciting at all.
##### [Pet](https://2e.aonprd.com/Feats.aspx?ID=5186)
You gain a pet that can't attack. Could be a useful scout.
##### [Ride](https://2e.aonprd.com/Feats.aspx?ID=5206)
Automatically succeed at commanding your mount to move. Not great.
##### [Shield Block](https://2e.aonprd.com/Feats.aspx?ID=5212)
Learn how to block with your shield.
##### [Toughness](https://2e.aonprd.com/Feats.aspx?ID=5227)
Max HP increase and lower DC of receovery checks. Is it exciting? No... For a rogue on the frontline this is handy to have.
##### [Weapon Proficiency](https://2e.aonprd.com/Feats.aspx?ID=5239)
Become trained in all martial weapons or if you're already trained in martial weapons, select 1 advanced weapon. Become an expert at level 11. 
##### [Ancestral Paragon](https://2e.aonprd.com/Feats.aspx?ID=5118)
Use your general feat slot for a level 1 ancestry feat. Could be worth it if you have a decent ancestry.
##### [Improvised Repair](https://2e.aonprd.com/Feats.aspx?ID=6486)
Quickly slap some flextape&trade; on that broken item and it can still be used until there is time to actually repair it.
##### [Keen Follower](https://2e.aonprd.com/Feats.aspx?ID=6490)
Monkey see, monkey do. Gain a circumstance bonus when using [Follow the Expert](https://2e.aonprd.com/Actions.aspx?ID=2625)
##### [Pick Up the Pace](https://2e.aonprd.com/Feats.aspx?ID=6495)
Push your group to [Hustle](https://2e.aonprd.com/Actions.aspx?ID=2626) for as long as the group member who can hustle the longest can.
##### [Prescient Planner](https://2e.aonprd.com/Feats.aspx?ID=5191)
Like a magician pulling a rabbit out of a hat, you can say you bought the exact item you needed and voila! You bought that last time you were in town, you just never mentioned it.
##### [Robust Health](https://2e.aonprd.com/Feats.aspx?ID=6499)
Gain a circumstance bonus to HP you regain from [Treat Wounds](https://2e.aonprd.com/Actions.aspx?ID=2399) or [Battle Medicine](https://2e.aonprd.com/Feats.aspx?ID=5125). Also only become immune to Battle Medicine for 1 hour instead of 1 day.
##### [Thorough Search](https://2e.aonprd.com/Feats.aspx?ID=6510)
No stone unturned... Take twice as long to [Search](https://2e.aonprd.com/Actions.aspx?ID=2630) an area and gain a +2 circumstance bonus to the check and a bump from a success to a critical success.
##### [Untrained Improvisation](https://2e.aonprd.com/Feats.aspx?ID=5233)
Gain a bonus to untrained skill checks. Still doesn't allow you to use the skill's trained actions.
##### [Expeditious Search](https://2e.aonprd.com/Feats.aspx?ID=5143)
Search an area in half the time, or quadruple the time if you're legendary in perception.
##### [Numb to Death](https://2e.aonprd.com/Feats.aspx?ID=6494)
I like the flavor here, only able to take it after dying once. Adds a bit of HP and you can ignore the [wounded](https://2e.aonprd.com/Conditions.aspx?ID=99) condition the first time you come back from dying each day. Maybe don't die so much?
##### [Prescient Consumable](https://2e.aonprd.com/Feats.aspx?ID=5190)
Able to get consumables with the [Precient Planner](https://2e.aonprd.com/Feats.aspx?ID=5191) feat.
##### [Supertaster](https://2e.aonprd.com/Feats.aspx?ID=6508)
Highly unlikely to be poisoned ever again. You can also lick or taste something while [investigating](https://2e.aonprd.com/Actions.aspx?ID=2627) or [recall knowledge](https://2e.aonprd.com/Actions.aspx?ID=2367) check to gain a +2 circumstance bonus if it would provide relevant additional information.
##### [A Home in Every Port](https://2e.aonprd.com/Feats.aspx?ID=6457)
Oozing with charisma, you secure lodging for up to 6 allies for 24 hours at no charge. Takes 8 hours of downtime.
##### [Caravan Leader](https://2e.aonprd.com/Feats.aspx?ID=6467)
You push your group to [Hustle](https://2e.aonprd.com/Actions.aspx?ID=2626) for 20 minutes beyond the limit of group member with the longest hustle time.
##### [Incredible Investiture](https://2e.aonprd.com/Feats.aspx?ID=5161)
You are so charismatic you can invest more magic items. Bumps up to a limit of 12 instead of 10.
##### [Incredible Scout](https://2e.aonprd.com/Feats.aspx?ID=6487)
When using the [Scout](https://2e.aonprd.com/Actions.aspx?ID=2629) exploration activity, you provide +2 circumstance bonus to allies' initiative instead of +1
##### [True Perception](https://2e.aonprd.com/Feats.aspx?ID=6511)
Nothing gets past those eagle eyes! You are constantly under the effect of a 6th rank [truesight spell](https://2e.aonprd.com/Spells.aspx?ID=1727)
