# Rogue Guide

## Introduction:
Few archetypes are as enduring as the rogue.

From the calculated patience of [Garrett](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Thief_(series)), to the lethal efficiency of [Arya Stark](https://awoiaf.westeros.org/index.php/Arya_Stark), to the silver-tongued audacity of [Locke Lamora](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/The_Lies_of_Locke_Lamora), the rogue fantasy is not about brute force. It is about leverage.

The rogue wins through positioning. Through timing. Through knowing something the enemy does not.

In **Pathfinder Second Edition**, the Rogue is one of the most tactically expressive classes in the system. It is not simply “the stealth class,” nor is it just a damage chassis for Sneak Attack. The Rogue is a precision striker, a battlefield opportunist, and arguably the most skill-dominant class in the game.

It rewards planning. It rewards awareness. It rewards system mastery.

And when piloted well, it is devastating.

---

### The Rogue’s True Role

At a glance, the Rogue appears straightforward:
- Apply [Off-Guard](https://2e.aonprd.com/Conditions.aspx?ID=58)
- Trigger [Sneak Attack](https://2e.aonprd.com/Classes.aspx?ID=37)
- Deal extra damage

But this surface-level reading undersells the class.

The Rogue’s true strength lies in action efficiency and information control. It exploits small advantages better than almost any other class. A flanked enemy is not merely easier to hit — it becomes a liability. A successful Recall Knowledge check is not flavor — it is a damage trigger. A Demoralize action is not filler — it is the beginning of a cascade.

Where other classes force advantage, the Rogue capitalizes on it.

This distinction matters.

Rogues are not independent damage engines in the way Fighters or Barbarians can be. They are synergy amplifiers. They turn teamwork into precision damage. They convert battlefield chaos into calculated execution.

If you enjoy reading the field, manipulating conditions, and squeezing value out of every action, the Rogue is one of the most rewarding classes to master.

---

### What This Guide Covers

This guide will examine:
- The Rogue’s mechanical identity and core features
- A breakdown of each racket (subclass) and what it enables
- Attribute priorities and build foundations
- Ancestry synergies
- Class and skill feat evaluation
- Archetype combinations that meaningfully expand the chassis
- Equipment priorities and scaling
- Several complete build concepts inspired by iconic rogue archetypes

The goal is not to recreate the rulebook. It is to interpret it.

Every recommendation here is grounded in practical table performance — not white-room damage math alone, but action economy, survivability, party synergy, and long-term scaling.

## Who Should Play a Rogue?

Play a Rogue if you:
- Enjoy tactical positioning and battlefield awareness
- Want the most skill flexibility in the system
- Prefer precision over brute force
- Like turning small advantages into decisive victories
- Want a class that scales with player mastery

Avoid the Rogue if you:
- Want to stand toe-to-toe and trade hits
- Prefer straightforward, repeatable rotations
- Dislike relying on positioning or team coordination

The Rogue thrives in capable hands. It punishes autopilot.

But for players willing to engage with its depth, it offers one of the most satisfying mechanical experiences in Pathfinder 2e.

#### [Scoundrel](https://2e.aonprd.com/Rackets.aspx?ID=8)
##### Overview
The suave, sleek, and charismatic ne'er-do-well. Maybe you enjoy doing the kessel run in as short a distance as you possibly can. Maybe you're heavily involved in the affairs of the court and you use your charming personality to influence rulings in your favor. There is a lot of room here. Scoundrels can [Feint](https://2e.aonprd.com/Actions.aspx?ID=48) and get a slightly better benefit than normal; success results in their remaining attacks against the target that round rather than their next attack, critical success results in the target being off-guard to all melee attacks until the end of your next turn (which is a huge benefit to the group). Also, if you're wielding a Finesse or Agile melee weapon, you can [Step](https://2e.aonprd.com/Actions.aspx?ID=87) as a free action after you feint, which can help with positioning.
##### Attributes
Key Stat: **CHA** 
- Aim for 16 in this to start
- DEX should be 18
- STR is needed for damage
- WIS or INT will need to be dumped/ignored
##### Granted Skills
- Deception
- Diplomacy
##### Combat Scenario
As a level 6 Rogue, you are moving through a dungeon corridor searching for traps with the [Search](https://2e.aonprd.com/Rules.aspx?ID=2598) exploration activity. A door 15 feet further up the corridor opens, a hulking [Ogre](https://2e.aonprd.com/Monsters.aspx?ID=3120) steps into the corridor and locks eyes with you. The GM calls for initiative to be rolled. With your high skill in perception you end up going first.

###### Opening
- `pf2:1` Move up to the Ogre without drawing a `pf2:r` [Reactive Strike](https://2e.aonprd.com/MonsterAbilities.aspx?ID=48) thanks to [Mobility](https://2e.aonprd.com/Feats.aspx?ID=4926)
- `pf2:1` [Distracting Feint](https://2e.aonprd.com/Feats.aspx?ID=4925) making the creature [Off-Guard](https://2e.aonprd.com/Conditions.aspx?ID=58) (hopefully to all melee attacks) and Step as your `pf2:0` free action to move to the side of the creature
- `pf2:1 Strike and if it hits you deal Sneak Attack which at level 6 is 2d6 extra damage

###### Considerations
- With the high CHA and training in Diplomacy/Deception, instead of feinting you could try to [Bon Mot](https://2e.aonprd.com/Feats.aspx?ID=6466) and reduce their Will DC for your casters in the party
	- This can be done from 30 feet, so you wouldn't need to get into melee
	- Then throw a weapon at them
	- If you grabbed Intimidation you could then try to [Demoralize](https://2e.aonprd.com/Actions.aspx?ID=2395) from a far

#### [Thief](https://2e.aonprd.com/Rackets.aspx?ID=9)
##### Overview
When you picture a Rogue, this racket is usually what you are picturing. The iconic burglar racket, nab that coin purse and slip into a shadowy alley. Or sneak into a dragon's lair and take the ~~Arkenstone~~ lovely gem from a copyright free dragon. 
The Thief is able to use their DEX as their Key stat **and** use it instead of STR for their damage with [finesse](https://2e.aonprd.com/Traits.aspx?ID=179) melee weapons.
##### Attributes
Key Stat: **DEX** 
- Aim for 18 in this to start
- STR can be safely dumped/ignored
##### Granted Skills
- Thievery
##### Combat Scenario
As a level 6 Rogue, you are moving through a dungeon corridor searching for traps with the [Search](https://2e.aonprd.com/Rules.aspx?ID=2598) exploration activity. A door 15 feet further up the corridor opens, a hulking [Ogre](https://2e.aonprd.com/Monsters.aspx?ID=3120) steps into the corridor and locks eyes with you. The GM calls for initiative to be rolled. With your high skill in perception you end up going first.

###### Opening
- `pf2:1` Move up to the Ogre without drawing a `pf2:r` [Reactive Strike](https://2e.aonprd.com/MonsterAbilities.aspx?ID=48) thanks to [Mobility](https://2e.aonprd.com/Feats.aspx?ID=4926)
- `pf2:0` Pull out your off-hand finesse melee weapon with [Quick Draw](https://2e.aonprd.com/Feats.aspx?ID=4869)
- `pf2:2` [Twin Feint](https://2e.aonprd.com/Feats.aspx?ID=4921) Strike with both weapons, with the second strike making the creature [Off-Guard](https://2e.aonprd.com/Conditions.aspx?ID=58) and if it hits you deal Sneak Attack which at level 6 is 2d6 extra damage.

###### Considerations
- Alternatively, you could adopt a hit-and-run tactic above which would be move up, strike, then move away which would mean the creature has to use at least one action to move back up to you
	- This can be risky if the creature has reactive strike (which in this scenario it does)
- Another option is to [Delay](https://2e.aonprd.com/Actions.aspx?ID=2294)
	- Also risky because you are the target and if the creature goes next, you get their ire
- One more: Use a thrown weapon for the first round and try to Recall Knowledge to help your party out

#### Avenger
You're on a mission from a god. You forsake the surprise attack feature and instead gain the [Hunt Prey](https://2e.aonprd.com/Actions.aspx?ID=2257) feature. You also get to use sneak attack with your diety's [favored weapon](https://2e.aonprd.com/Rules.aspx?ID=805), which can be powerful. You become trained in medium armor and can choose STR as your key stat. You also must take the [Avenger Dedication](https://2e.aonprd.com/Feats.aspx?ID=7232) at level 2

##### Attributes
Key Stat: **STR**
- Get this up to 18 to start
- Get DEX up to at least 12
- WIS up to 14
- CHA up to 12
##### Granted Skills
- Diety's divine skill
- Intimidation
- Religion
- Stealth
##### Combat Scenario
As a level 6 Rogue, you are moving through a dungeon corridor searching for traps with the [Search](https://2e.aonprd.com/Rules.aspx?ID=2598) exploration activity. A door 15 feet further up the corridor opens, a hulking [Ogre](https://2e.aonprd.com/Monsters.aspx?ID=3120) steps into the corridor and locks eyes with you. The GM calls for initiative to be rolled. With your high skill in perception you end up going first.

###### Opening
- `pf2:1` You narrow your eyes and mentally focus on the creature, marking it as your prey. (Hunt Prey)
- `pf2:1` You stride forth with your diety's favored weapon drawn potentially drawing a reactive strike from the creature
- `pf2:1` You Strike out with the power of your god [Zealous Inevitability](https://2e.aonprd.com/Feats.aspx?ID=7233) dealing 2d6 from sneak attack

###### Considerations
- There are feats from the [Ranger](https://2e.aonprd.com/Classes.aspx?ID=36) class which allow you to wield two weapons quite easily
- You could focus on a Demoralize build and pick up Dread Striker to get creature's Off-Guard
- If you go for one weapon, aim for a diety that grants a weapon with decent damage
- With the focus on STR, you could go for an athletics maneuver build and grapple or trip (or get a favored weapon with those traits) and be a boon to your party
- Although this Racket gets you into the thick of it and makes you a bit beefier, you are not a tank, consider using hit-and-run tactics

#### [Mastermind](https://2e.aonprd.com/Rackets.aspx?ID=6)
Ah, the elusive figure hidden in shadow that controls the creatures on the battlefield like pawns. With your high intellect and class features you want to focus on [Recall Knowledge](https://2e.aonprd.com/Actions.aspx?ID=26) which when successful causes the creature to be Off-Guard to your attacks until the start of your next turn or on a critical success; it is Off-Guard for the next minute. 
##### Attributes
Key Stat: **INT**
- Get this to at least 16
- Get DEX to 18
- Get WIS to 14
- Ignore/Dump STR or CHA
##### Granted Skills
- Society
- Choice of: Arcana, Nature, Religion, Occultism
##### Combat Scenario
As a level 6 Rogue, you are moving through a dungeon corridor searching for traps with the [Search](https://2e.aonprd.com/Rules.aspx?ID=2598) exploration activity. A door 15 feet further up the corridor opens, a hulking [Ogre](https://2e.aonprd.com/Monsters.aspx?ID=3120) steps into the corridor and locks eyes with you. The GM calls for initiative to be rolled. With your high skill in perception you end up going first.

###### Opening
- `pf2:1` You narrow your eyes and attempt a Recall Knowledge check on the creature. It is a humanoid so your Society skill comes in handy and you succeed, causing the Ogre to be off-guard against your attacks until the start of your next turn. It also triggers [Analyze Weakness](https://2e.aonprd.com/Feats.aspx?ID=4940)
- `pf2:0` You draw your bow and knock an arrow with [Quick Draw](https://2e.aonprd.com/Feats.aspx?ID=4869) as a free action
- `pf2:1` You Strike with your bow dealing your regular 2d6 from sneak attack, plus an additional 2d6 from analyze weakness
  - `pf2:1` You Strike an additional time dealing 2d6 from sneak attack
  
###### Considerations
- The potential to play this racket in melee is there, it would mean moving up first, potentially getting struck, then recalling knowledge and striking
- Thrown weapons and melee weapons could work, but you would need to get your damage from a higher STR stat, so you can't dump it
- Pick up [Dread Striker](https://2e.aonprd.com/Feats.aspx?ID=4930) and convince one of your group mates to always demoralize as a bit of insurance for when you don't succeed on your recall knowledge checks

#### [Ruffian](https://2e.aonprd.com/Rackets.aspx?ID=7)
The bully racket, push and shove others around to get what you want. You can use any simple weapon with a damage die of d8 or lower, or a martial/advanced weapon with a damage die of d6 or lower to deal sneak attack damage. You also gain training in medium armor which is helpful since we'll be focusing on STR instead of DEX.
##### Attributes
Key Stat: **STR**
- Get this to 18 to start
- Get DEX up to at least 14
- Get CHA up to at least 14 as well
- Dealer's choice for the other attributes, but INT is probably what you will dump/ignore
##### Granted Skills
- Intimidation
##### Combat Scenario
As a level 6 Rogue, you are moving through a dungeon corridor searching for traps with the [Search](https://2e.aonprd.com/Rules.aspx?ID=2598) exploration activity. A door 15 feet further up the corridor opens, a hulking [Ogre](https://2e.aonprd.com/Monsters.aspx?ID=3120) steps into the corridor and locks eyes with you. The GM calls for initiative to be rolled. With your high skill in perception you end up going first.

###### Opening
- `pf2:1` Move up to the Ogre and draw a `pf2:r` [Reactive Strike](https://2e.aonprd.com/MonsterAbilities.aspx?ID=48) for moving into it's reach, pull the Uno reverse with your own `pf2:r` reaction [Nimble Dodge](https://2e.aonprd.com/Feats.aspx?ID=4916) causing the attack to miss
- `pf2:1` [Demoralize](https://2e.aonprd.com/Actions.aspx?ID=2395) the creature with your excellent skill in Intimidate and cause it to be [Frightened](https://2e.aonprd.com/Conditions.aspx?ID=76)
- `pf2:1 Strike and if it hits you deal Sneak Attack damage which at level 6 is 2d6 extra damage because of [Dread Striker](https://2e.aonprd.com/Feats.aspx?ID=4930)

###### Considerations
- With the high STR you could instead focus on athletics maneuvers such as [Grapple](https://2e.aonprd.com/Actions.aspx?ID=2376) which can make the creature Off-Guard
- Alternatively, you could play it a bit safer, a bit more cautiously, and grab [Strong Arm](https://2e.aonprd.com/Feats.aspx?ID=4927) to gain a bit more range for thrown weapons since their damage is based on STR 

## Ancestries
Aside from your class this is the core of your character. I'm going to be rating these under the assumption that you aren't using alternative ancestry boots or
the [optional rule](https://2e.aonprd.com/Rules.aspx?ID=2033) voluntary flaws. As I go through these I'm looking at the base stat values to see how well they mesh with the stat requirements of the subclass, there are other considerations, such as the ancestry feats, however, most are "nice-to-haves" not "must-haves". Once you've found a couple of ancestries that you like, delve into their feats to see if you are swayed to one side or the other.

## Skill Feats
Rogues gain a skill feat at 1st level and every level after. There is a reason why they are considered skill monkeys. Sadly, skill feats may be the weakest part of Second Edition, many don't have a Legendary capstone skill, and the majority of feats are incredibly narrow-scoped.

## General Feats
A level 20 character will have 5 general feats.
### Three Tomes
<div class="not-prose">
  {{< figure
    src="/images/tome3.png"
    alt="Three Tomes"
    class="w-25"
  >}}
</div>

#### [Fleet](https://2e.aonprd.com/Feats.aspx?ID=5150)
Gain an extra 5 feet of speed. Not exciting, but hey, it helps to get into position and interacts with [Mobility](https://2e.aonprd.com/Feats.aspx?ID=4926) and [Sneak](https://2e.aonprd.com/Actions.aspx?ID=2405).
#### [Toughness](https://2e.aonprd.com/Feats.aspx?ID=5227)
Max HP increase and lower DC of recovery checks. Is it exciting? No... For a rogue on the front-line this is handy to have.
#### [Diehard](https://2e.aonprd.com/Feats.aspx?ID=5140)
Die at dying 5, rather than dying 4. Handy if you're in melee.
#### [Adopted Ancestry](https://2e.aonprd.com/Feats.aspx?ID=5115)
Learn to be apart of another ancestry you have access to. Can select those ancestry feats as well as your original ancestry. Can lead to some fun shenanigans and strong interactions.
#### [True Perception](https://2e.aonprd.com/Feats.aspx?ID=6511)
Nothing gets past those eagle eyes! You are constantly under the effect of a 6th rank [truesight spell](https://2e.aonprd.com/Spells.aspx?ID=1727). Magical sight, sign up today for the ocular implants of the future!
#### [Feather Step](https://2e.aonprd.com/Feats.aspx?ID=5149)
Gain the ability to Step into difficult terrain. Better a general feat than a class feat.

### Two Tomes
<div class="not-prose">
  {{< figure
    src="/images/tome2.png"
    alt="Two Tomes"
    class="w-25"
  >}}
</div>

#### [Ancestral Paragon](https://2e.aonprd.com/Feats.aspx?ID=5118)
Use your general feat slot for a level 1 ancestry feat. Could be worth it if you have a decent ancestry.
#### [Incredible Investiture](https://2e.aonprd.com/Feats.aspx?ID=5161)
You are so charismatic you can invest more magic items. Bumps up to a limit of 12 instead of 10. If you have the charisma for it, and you're in a campaign with a plethora of magic items, go for it.
#### [Pet](https://2e.aonprd.com/Feats.aspx?ID=5186)
You gain a pet that can't attack. Could be a useful scout.
#### [Breath Control](https://2e.aonprd.com/Feats.aspx?ID=5129)
Hold your breath longer and gain benefits against inhaled threats.  Maybe if there will be water?
#### [Fast Recovery](https://2e.aonprd.com/Feats.aspx?ID=5148)
Recover from disease faster, gain more HP from resting. Make friends with your healer instead.
#### [Prescient Planner](https://2e.aonprd.com/Feats.aspx?ID=5191)
Like a magician pulling a rabbit out of a hat, you can say you bought the exact item you needed and voila! You bought that last time you were in town, you just never mentioned it. Might be worth it instead of using a class feat for [Predictive Purchase](https://2e.aonprd.com/Feats.aspx?ID=4955), though that gives you both feats for one.
#### [Prescient Consumable](https://2e.aonprd.com/Feats.aspx?ID=5190)
Able to get consumables with the [Prescient Planner](https://2e.aonprd.com/Feats.aspx?ID=5191) feat.
#### [Robust Health](https://2e.aonprd.com/Feats.aspx?ID=6499)
Gain a circumstance bonus to HP you regain from [Treat Wounds](https://2e.aonprd.com/Actions.aspx?ID=2399) or [Battle Medicine](https://2e.aonprd.com/Feats.aspx?ID=5125). Also only become immune to Battle Medicine for 1 hour instead of 1 day. Most medic skills reduce the cooldown anyways, this doesn't seem worth it.
#### [Numb to Death](https://2e.aonprd.com/Feats.aspx?ID=6494)
I like the flavor here, only able to take it after dying once. Adds a bit of HP and you can ignore the [wounded](https://2e.aonprd.com/Conditions.aspx?ID=99) condition the first time you come back from dying each day. Maybe don't die so much? It's fun flavor, but there are better choices.

### One Tome
<div class="not-prose">
  {{< figure
    src="/images/tome1.png"
    alt="One Tome"
    class="w-25"
  >}}
</div>

#### [Armor Proficiency](https://2e.aonprd.com/Feats.aspx?ID=5120)
Become trained in the next tier of armor, then at level 13 become an expert. Whichever racket you choose will have the correct armor proficiency for your attributes, not need for this.
#### [Canny Acumen](https://2e.aonprd.com/Feats.aspx?ID=5130)
Become an expert in a saving throw or Perception. Useful for Fortitude saves at level 17... that's a long time for this feat to become worth it.
#### [Incredible Initiative](https://2e.aonprd.com/Feats.aspx?ID=5160)
Gain a +2 circumstance bonus to initiative rolls. Not exciting at all and it is a circumstance bonus which generally means that someone else will be providing one.
#### [Ride](https://2e.aonprd.com/Feats.aspx?ID=5206)
Automatically succeed at commanding your mount to move. If you want a mount go for the [Animal Trainer](https://2e.aonprd.com/Archetypes.aspx?ID=38) archetype and a small/medium race.
#### [Shield Block](https://2e.aonprd.com/Feats.aspx?ID=5212)
Learn how to block with your shield. You want to use your reaction for other things.
#### [Weapon Proficiency](https://2e.aonprd.com/Feats.aspx?ID=5239)
Become trained in all martial weapons or if you're already trained in martial weapons, select 1 advanced weapon. Become an expert at level 11. Grab an ancestry that gives the weapon you want.
##### [Improvised Repair](https://2e.aonprd.com/Feats.aspx?ID=6486)
Quickly slap some flextape&trade; on that broken item and it can still be used until there is time to actually repair it. There is a skill feat that is much better than this.
#### [Keen Follower](https://2e.aonprd.com/Feats.aspx?ID=6490)
Monkey see, monkey do. Gain a circumstance bonus when using [Follow the Expert](https://2e.aonprd.com/Actions.aspx?ID=2625) Everyone should be following you, you're the skill monkey.
#### [Pick Up the Pace](https://2e.aonprd.com/Feats.aspx?ID=6495)
Push your group to [Hustle](https://2e.aonprd.com/Actions.aspx?ID=2626) for as long as the group member who can hustle the longest can. Not worth a feat to move faster for a few minutes.
#### [Thorough Search](https://2e.aonprd.com/Feats.aspx?ID=6510)
No stone unturned... Take twice as long to [Search](https://2e.aonprd.com/Actions.aspx?ID=2630) an area and gain a +2 circumstance bonus to the check and a bump from a success to a critical success. You have great perception, I don't think this is necessary.
#### [Untrained Improvisation](https://2e.aonprd.com/Feats.aspx?ID=5233)
Gain a bonus to untrained skill checks. Still doesn't allow you to use the skill's trained actions. You're a rogue with skills for days, you don't need this.
#### [Expeditious Search](https://2e.aonprd.com/Feats.aspx?ID=5143)
Search an area in half the time, or quadruple the time if you're legendary in perception. Maybe if you're in a garbage compactor and the walls are closing in?
#### [Supertaster](https://2e.aonprd.com/Feats.aspx?ID=6508)
Highly unlikely to be poisoned ever again. You can also lick or taste something while [investigating](https://2e.aonprd.com/Actions.aspx?ID=2627) or [recall knowledge](https://2e.aonprd.com/Actions.aspx?ID=2367) check to gain a +2 circumstance bonus if it would provide relevant additional information. Leave the wall licking to the investigator.
#### [A Home in Every Port](https://2e.aonprd.com/Feats.aspx?ID=6457)
Oozing with charisma, you secure lodging for up to 6 allies for 24 hours at no charge. Takes 8 hours of downtime. It sounds fun, but just pay someone the 2 gold and save 8 hours of downtime for something else.
#### [Caravan Leader](https://2e.aonprd.com/Feats.aspx?ID=6467)
You push your group to [Hustle](https://2e.aonprd.com/Actions.aspx?ID=2626) for 20 minutes beyond the limit of group member with the longest hustle time. I have no words.
#### [Incredible Scout](https://2e.aonprd.com/Feats.aspx?ID=6487)
When using the [Scout](https://2e.aonprd.com/Actions.aspx?ID=2629) exploration activity, you provide +2 circumstance bonus to allies' initiative instead of +1. I don't like this one, another circumstance bonus in a sea of circumstance bonuses.
